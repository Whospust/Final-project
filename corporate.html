About Sony Corporation

Sony was founded in 1946 by Masaru Ibuka and Akio Morita. Originally named Tokyo Tsushin Kogyo, the company changed its name to Sony in 1958. The name is derived from "Sonus," the Latin word for sound, and "Sonny," a slang term for a young boy.
